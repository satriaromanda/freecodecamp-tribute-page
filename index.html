Abdurrahman Wahid

"Saya tidak peduli, mau popularitas saya hancur, difitnah, dicaci maki, atau dituduh apapun, tapi bangsa dan negara ini harus diselamatkan dari perpecahan"
GusDur

Abdurrahman Wahid was born at 7 September 1940, colloquaially known as GusDur. He was an Indonesian Muslim religious and political leader who served as the fourth president of Indonesia from 1999 to 2001. The long-time president of the Nahdlatul Ulama and the founder of the National Awakening Party (PKB), GusDur was the first elected president of Indonesia after the resignation of Suharto in 1998.
  • 1993 : GusDur received the prestigious Magsaysay Award for his efforts to promote inter-religious relations in Indonesia within a democratic society. The award is referred to as 'Asia's Nobel Prize'.
  • 2001 : Left from the Presidential Palace on 25 July.
  • 2003 : GusDur awarded the World Peace Prize Top Honor by WPPAC, for facilitating democratization of Indonesia and preserving peace and stability through inter-religious collaborations and inter-racial communications.
